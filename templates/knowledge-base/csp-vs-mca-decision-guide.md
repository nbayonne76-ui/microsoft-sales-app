# CSP vs MCA Decision Guide

## 🎯 Quick Selection Framework

This guide helps you choose between **CSP Indirect** and **MCA (Microsoft Customer Agreement)** based on customer profile and needs.

---

## Option 1: CSP Indirect (Cloud Solution Provider)

**Price**: Negotiable with partner | **Support**: Via partner

### ✅ Choose CSP Indirect When:

#### 🤝 **You Value Proximity and Support**
- ✅ **Need regular technical support**: Partner provides hands-on assistance
- ✅ **Prefer a single point of contact**: Partner acts as your trusted advisor
- ✅ **Want proactive and personalized support**: Relationship-focused service

#### 🏢 **Your Organizational Profile**
- ✅ **SME/Mid-market company**: Even if EA-eligible (250+ users)
- ✅ **Medium-sized IT team**: Need help with provisioning and daily management
- ✅ **Need help with daily management and provisioning**: Not fully self-service

#### 💰 **Budget Flexibility Matters**
- ✅ **Ability to lock pricing for 3 years** (for certain SKUs)
  - Example: Microsoft 365 Standard licenses
  - Protection against annual price increases

- ✅ **Annual commitment by default** (less constraining than EA)
- ✅ **Monthly or annual billing** at your choice

#### 🎯 **Current Context**
- ✅ **Already have a trusted partner** (Sword, or similar)
- ✅ **Need support for tenant merger**: Technical expertise required
- ✅ **Technical projects requiring expertise**: Complex migrations, hybrid setups

#### ⚠️ **Important: October 2025**
Verify that your partner meets the **new security requirements** (MFA, GDAP, compliance)

---

## Option 2: MCA (Microsoft Customer Agreement)

**Price**: No expiration, market-based | **Support**: Direct Microsoft

### ✅ Choose MCA When:

#### ☁️ **High Cloud Maturity**
- ✅ **Autonomous and experienced IT team** on Azure + M365
  - Self-sufficient with Microsoft cloud services
  - Experienced Azure architects on staff
  - Minimal need for external support

- ✅ **Capacity to manage directly with Microsoft**
  - Comfortable navigating Admin Center + Cost Management
  - Able to handle Microsoft support tickets
  - Experience with DSI (Designated Support Engineer) model

- ✅ **Experienced DSI** (e.g., Antoine Laporte, DSI since March)
  - Long-term Microsoft relationship
  - Deep understanding of Microsoft licensing
  - Strategic technology leadership

#### 📋 **Administrative Simplification**
- ✅ **One single contract** (vs. multiple EA documents)
  - Streamlined paperwork
  - Faster procurement process

- ✅ **12 pages** instead of 24+ for EA
  - Simplified terms and conditions
  - Easier legal review

- ✅ **Centralized digital management** (Admin Center + Cost Management)
  - Real-time visibility into all subscriptions
  - Unified billing portal
  - Self-service license management

#### ☁️ **Azure + M365 Unification**
- ✅ **Already have an important Azure tenant**
  - Significant Azure infrastructure investment
  - Multiple Azure subscriptions
  - Dataonflake projects in progress on Azure

- ✅ **Snowflake data projects in progress on Azure**
  - Modern data platform initiatives
  - Cloud-native development

- ✅ **Need for consolidated visibility on all cloud consumption**
  - Unified cost management
  - Chargeback/showback capabilities
  - Budget tracking across services

#### 🚀 **Maximum Flexibility**
- ✅ **No contract expiration** (unlike EA with 3-year commitment)
  - Add services anytime
  - No renewal negotiations
  - Continuous flexibility

- ✅ **Add services on demand without prior completion**
  - Start small, scale as needed
  - No large upfront commitments

- ✅ **Evolution with business needs**
  - Adapt to changing requirements
  - No penalty for growth or reduction

#### 💰 **Financial Transparency**
- ✅ **Real-time cost visibility**
  - Azure Cost Management + Billing (free included)
  - Daily cost tracking
  - Granular resource-level insights

- ✅ **Native Azure Cost Management**
  - Built-in budgets and alerts
  - Cost recommendations from Azure Advisor
  - Forecasting and trend analysis

- ✅ **Unified billing**
  - Single invoice for Azure + M365 + Dynamics
  - Consolidated payment
  - Simplified accounting

## 💼 Real-World Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Small Business (50-100 users)
**Profile:**
- 50 employees
- Small IT team (1-2 people)
- Limited Azure usage
- Need guidance on M365 setup

**Recommendation:** ✅ **CSP Indirect**

**Why:**
- Partner support is valuable for small teams
- Technical guidance during setup and growth
- Predictable pricing with 3-year lock option
- Single point of contact simplifies management

**Cost Example:**
- 50 × M365 Business Premium @ $22 = $1,100/month
- Partner margin: ~10-15%
- Total: ~$1,200-1,265/month
- Value: Partner support included

---

### Scenario 2: Mid-Market with Growing Azure (200 users)
**Profile:**
- 200 employees
- 3-4 person IT team
- Growing Azure consumption ($5,000/month)
- Mix of M365 and Azure needs

**Recommendation:** ⚖️ **Either works, but trending toward MCA**

**Why CSP could work:**
- Partner relationship for complex issues
- Negotiable pricing
- Consolidated billing through partner

**Why MCA is better:**
- Unified Azure + M365 management
- Native cost visibility
- Direct Microsoft support for Azure
- No partner margin on Azure consumption

**Cost Comparison:**
```
CSP:
- M365: 200 × $36 (E3) = $7,200/month
- Azure: $5,000/month (via partner, ~10% margin)
- Total: ~$13,420/month

MCA:
- M365: 200 × $36 (E3) = $7,200/month
- Azure: $5,000/month (direct, no margin)
- Total: $12,200/month
- Savings: $1,220/month or $14,640/year
```

---

### Scenario 3: Enterprise with Heavy Azure (500+ users)
**Profile:**
- 500+ employees
- Experienced IT and Azure team
- Significant Azure spend ($50,000+/month)
- Data projects (Snowflake, analytics)
- DSI with Microsoft experience

**Recommendation:** ✅ **MCA**

**Why:**
- **Cost savings**: No partner margin on large Azure spend
- **Unified management**: Single platform for all resources
- **Financial transparency**: Real-time cost tracking
- **Direct support**: Fast escalation for critical issues
- **Strategic relationship**: Direct Microsoft engagement

**Cost Comparison:**
```
CSP:
- M365 E3: 500 × $36 = $18,000/month
- Azure: $50,000/month × 1.10 (margin) = $55,000/month
- Total: $73,000/month or $876,000/year

MCA:
- M365 E3: 500 × $36 = $18,000/month
- Azure: $50,000/month (no margin)
- Total: $68,000/month or $816,000/year
- **Annual savings: $60,000**
```

---

### Scenario 4: Hybrid Approach (Starting Small)
**Profile:**
- 300 users on CSP currently
- Starting Azure adoption
- Want to test MCA without full commitment

**Recommendation:** 🔄 **Progressive Migration**

**Strategy:**
1. **Keep CSP for M365** (existing licenses)
2. **Test MCA for new Azure projects** (limited scope)
3. **Evaluate for 3-6 months**
4. **Decide on full migration** based on experience

**Benefits:**
- Low-risk evaluation
- Compare support models directly
- Test Azure Cost Management tools
- Learn MCA portal without pressure

## 🔄 Migration Paths

### CSP → MCA Migration

#### When to Migrate:
- ✅ CSP contract expiring
- ✅ Azure consumption growing significantly (>$10,000/month)
- ✅ IT team now experienced enough for self-management
- ✅ Want direct Microsoft relationship

#### Migration Process:
1. **Evaluation Phase** (1-2 months)
   - Analyze current CSP costs and partner value
   - Compare with MCA pricing
   - Assess IT team readiness

2. **Partner Discussion**
   - Some partners can facilitate MCA transition
   - Negotiate exit terms
   - Discuss potential continued services

3. **Pilot Period** (3-6 months recommended)
   - Start with Azure workloads on MCA
   - Keep M365 on CSP initially
   - Test support and management experience

4. **Full Migration** (if pilot successful)
   - Transfer M365 licenses
   - Consolidate billing
   - Close CSP relationship or renegotiate scope

#### Considerations:
- ⚠️ **Partner relationship**: May lose valuable support
- ⚠️ **Learning curve**: Direct Microsoft support is different
- ⚠️ **Pricing**: May be higher or lower depending on negotiation
- ✅ **Flexibility**: Gain more control and transparency

---

### MCA → CSP Migration (Rare but possible)

#### When to Consider:
- ❌ Overwhelmed by direct Microsoft management
- ❌ Need more hands-on support
- ❌ IT team turnover/expertise loss
- ❌ Complex technical projects requiring partner help

#### Why it's rare:
- Most companies gain expertise over time
- MCA flexibility is hard to give up
- Cost savings on Azure are significant

## ⚠️ Important Considerations

### 🔒 **Security & Compliance (October 2025)**

#### CSP Partners Must:
- ✅ Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all admins
- ✅ Use Granular Delegated Admin Privileges (GDAP) instead of legacy DAP
- ✅ Meet enhanced security requirements
- ✅ Pass Microsoft authorization checks

**Action Items:**
- Verify partner compliance **before** October 2025
- Request security compliance documentation
- Ensure smooth transition to GDAP
- Validate partner still authorized post-October

### 💰 **Pricing Considerations**

#### CSP Advantages:
- ✅ **3-year price lock** possible (certain SKUs)
  - Protection against annual increases
  - Budget predictability

- ✅ **Partner negotiation**
  - Volume discounts
  - Competitive quotes from multiple partners

#### MCA Advantages:
- ✅ **No partner margin** on Azure (5-15% savings)
- ✅ **Market pricing** for M365
- ✅ **Negotiable with Microsoft** for large deployments
- ✅ **Azure Reserved Instances** (up to 72% savings)

### 📊 **Support Model Differences**

#### CSP Support:
- 👤 **Partner as first contact**
  - Faster for known issues
  - Local language and timezone
  - Relationship-based

- 🔄 **Partner escalates to Microsoft** when needed
  - Two-tier support model
  - May take longer for complex issues

- 🛠️ **Proactive management** often included
  - Monitoring and alerts
  - Regular health checks
  - Best practices guidance

#### MCA Support:
- 📞 **Direct Microsoft access**
  - Single-tier support
  - Faster escalation for critical issues

- 🌐 **24/7 global support** (with paid plans)
  - Standard: $100/month
  - Professional Direct: $1,000/month
  - Premier/Unified: Custom pricing

- 🤖 **Self-service tools**
  - Extensive documentation
  - Community forums
  - AI-powered support chatbot

## 🎯 Key Takeaways

### Choose **CSP Indirect** if you:
1. ✅ Value partner relationship and local support
2. ✅ Have small/medium IT team needing help
3. ✅ Want 3-year price lock (where available)
4. ✅ Prefer single point of contact
5. ✅ Have complex migrations or technical projects
6. ✅ Limited Azure consumption (<$10,000/month)

### Choose **MCA** if you:
1. ✅ Have experienced, autonomous IT team
2. ✅ High Azure consumption (>$10,000/month)
3. ✅ Want unified Azure + M365 management
4. ✅ Need real-time cost visibility
5. ✅ Prefer direct Microsoft relationship
6. ✅ Value contractual simplicity (12 pages vs 24)
7. ✅ Want maximum flexibility (no expiration)

### ⚖️ **Hybrid Approach**:
- Test MCA with Azure workloads first
- Keep M365 on CSP initially
- Migrate fully after successful pilot
- Best of both worlds during transition

## 📞 Getting Started

### Next Steps for CSP:
1. Research authorized CSP partners
2. Request quotes from 2-3 partners
3. Compare pricing, services, and support offerings
4. Check partner security compliance (October 2025)
5. Select partner and sign agreement

### Next Steps for MCA:
1. Contact Microsoft account manager
2. Request MCA contract and pricing
3. Evaluate Azure Cost Management tools
4. Assess IT team readiness for self-management
5. Plan migration timeline if coming from CSP/EA

### Questions to Ask Partners/Microsoft:
- What are the exact pricing terms?
- What support response times are guaranteed?
- How do you handle billing disputes?
- What happens if I need to migrate later?
- Can you provide customer references?
- What training/onboarding is included?
