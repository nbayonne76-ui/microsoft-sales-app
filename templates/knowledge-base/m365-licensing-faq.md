## Original SKUs vs. Unified SKUs

**Q7. Which Microsoft 365 suites have Unified SKUs?**
- Microsoft 365 E3 (commercial, WW Gov, GCC-High, DoD)
- Microsoft 365 E5 (commercial, WW Gov, GCC-High, DoD)
- Microsoft 365 G5 GCC
- Microsoft 365 F3 GCC
- Microsoft 365 A3 (Faculty, Student, Student Use Benefit)
- Microsoft 365 A5 (Faculty, Student, Student Use Benefit)

*Note: Existing customers should continue using Original SKUs for existing subscriptions. Transition to Unified requires manual unassign/reassign of licenses.*

**Q8. What is the difference between Original SKUs and Unified SKUs?**

| Original SKUs | Unified SKUs |
|---|---|
| Individual components (O365, Windows Enterprise, EMS) in Admin Center | Single product in Admin Center |
| Individual components appear separately in License Summary | Online services appear as single component in License Summary |
| Multiple product IDs to manage | Single license assignment |

**Q9. What happens when a customer with an Original SKU needs to change licenses?**

| Customer scenario | SKU guideline |
|---|---|
| Renew existing subscriptions | Move to Unified SKUs at renewal (preferred), or renew Original SKUs as-is |
| Add seats mid-term | Use existing SKU |
| Step-up mid-term | Use Original Step-up SKUs for Original licenses, Unified Step-up for Unified licenses |
| New subscription/new tenant | Unified SKUs only |

**Q10. Can a customer transition from Original to Unified? Can Microsoft support them?**
Yes. The customer must manually assign the Unified SKU licenses to their users.

**Q11. How to differentiate Original vs. Unified on pricelists?**
Look at the SKU description and/or PFAM name : explicitly labeled "Original" or "Unified."

**Q12. What about mid-term transitions from Original to Unified?**
Mid-term transitions require a revised CPS with credit/rebill and are highly discouraged.

**Q13. The Original and Unified SKUs have different prices in my local pricelist. Is this an error?**
No. USD prices are the same. Some currencies differ because FX rates are calculated differently for Original vs. Unified SKUs.

**Q14. Why is the FX rate calculated differently for Unified SKUs?**
O365, EMS, and Windows have different FX rates. Unified SKUs must use a single "blended" rate that approximates the separate rates but cannot match them perfectly across all currencies.

**Q15. Why can the percentage difference appear large on Add-On SKUs between Original and Unified?**
Add-on pricing is a differential between two SKUs. Any change in either the higher or lower SKU shifts the add-on price. On a percentage basis, the impact can appear large even if the dollar amount is small.

**Q16. Can a customer on Original SKU purchase Unified at renewal if Unified is priced lower?**
Yes. Customers can move to Unified at renewal if they accept the administrative burden of manually transitioning users.

**Q17. Can I discount Original SKU to match Unified if customer refuses to switch?**
Yes. Use standard Field Empowerment levels to discount the Original SKU to match Unified pricing.

**Q18. Can I quote an Original SKU to new customers if it's priced lower than Unified?**
No. Only Unified SKUs should be quoted for new subscriptions or tenants.

**Q19. Does the Original/Unified change affect CSP customers?**
No. Only EA, EAS, MPSA, and EES customers are impacted.

**Q20. Is there any difference in product features between Original and Unified SKUs?**
No.

**Q21. Can a customer have both Original and Unified SKUs on their tenant?**
Yes.

**Q22. What licenses are available in M365 Admin Portal for M365 E3 Original users?**
- Office 365 E3
- Enterprise Mobility + Security E3
- Microsoft 365 E3 Extra Features
- Microsoft Defender for Endpoint P1

**Q23. What licenses are available for M365 E5 Original users?**

- Enterprise Mobility + Security E5

*Note: Windows E3 licenses in M365 E3/E5 Original are on-premises and do not appear in the portal as user subscription licenses.*

## Microsoft 365 Pricing Updates : Context & Policy

**Q25. Which SKUs were impacted by the March 2022 price update?**
- Enterprise: Office 365 E1, Office 365 E3, Office 365 E5, Microsoft 365 E3
- SMB: Microsoft 365 Business Basic, Microsoft 365 Business Premium
- Not changed: Microsoft 365 E5, Business Standard, Frontline SKUs, standalones

**Q26. Why did Microsoft increase the price in 2022?**
First substantive price update since Office 365 launched in June 2011. Reflects 23 new apps and 1,400+ features added over 10 years, including Teams, DLP, security capabilities, and AI features.

**Q27. How were Government customers impacted?**
Price increases for Government customers were implemented in a phased manner due to broader GOV price rationalization and a 10% annual price increase cap (from March 2022 through March 2025 depending on the SKU).

**Q28. Were Non-profit plans also updated?**
Yes. Non-profit/charitable plan prices were updated starting September 1, 2022. No changes applied to Microsoft 365 E5, Business Standard, Frontline, or standalone SKUs.

**Q29. Are there regional exceptions to price increases?**
Yes. The Office 365 E1 compete SKU in India was excluded from the price increase. For Microsoft 365 Business Basic, Brazil, Mexico, and India had no price increase, and Malaysia saw a price reduction (available on CSP and Web Direct).

**Q30. Does a price change affect both monthly and annual billing?**
Yes. Both billing options are impacted at the next renewal following the price change effective date.

**Q31. Policy on Early Commit to avoid a price increase?**
- Sellers should NOT lead with Early Commit or imply it avoids the price increase absent an upsell
- Available empowerment: use prior-month price list, early commits/renewals for a max of 3 months
- Do NOT use prior-month price lists solely to avoid the increase
- Early Renewals: only if specifically requested by the customer for M&A/divestiture scenarios
- Deals requiring Early Commit beyond 3 months must be escalated via CE to Deal Desk

**Q32. Are there any changes to licensing or usage rights with a price update?**
No, unless explicitly stated in the announcement.

**Q33. Which licensing programs do price updates apply to?**
Commercial Volume Licensing, CSP, MPSA, and Web Direct.

**Q34. Are Education or Consumer prices impacted by commercial price updates?**
No.

**Q35. What new value justifies the price evolution of Microsoft 365?**
Since Office 365 launched in June 2011, Microsoft has added 24 new apps and over 1,400 features:
- Microsoft Teams (meetings, chats, calls, collaboration, workflow automation)
- Real-time collaboration (co-authoring, @mentions, modern comments, auto-save)
- Security capabilities: DLP for documents and emails, conditional access, messaging encryption, attack surface reduction
- Microsoft Defender for Endpoint Plan 1 (next gen protection, ASR, M365 Defender console)
- AI capabilities: Editor, Researcher, Designer, Translation

**Q36. Will Microsoft raise prices again in the future?**
Microsoft regularly evaluates pricing based on value delivered to customers. The 2022 update was the first substantive change in over a decade but does not constitute a "once every 10 years" commitment.

## Universal Print

**Q42. What is Universal Print?**
A cloud-based print solution managed via Microsoft cloud services : no on-premises print servers required.

**Q43. Is Universal Print included in existing plans?**

| Suite | Jobs included per month |
|---|---|
| Microsoft 365 E3/E5 | 100 per licensed user (from May 1, 2023) |
| Windows E3/A3/E5/A5, M365 Business Premium/F3/A3/A5 | 5 per licensed user |

Student licenses receive access rights to print using Universal Print but no monthly print capacity. Their print jobs count against the faculty users' pool.

**Q44. Is there a Volume Add-on available?**
Yes. Two tiers available (500 jobs/month and 10,000 jobs/month) as add-on subscriptions for tenants needing more than the included capacity.

**Q45. Through which channels is Universal Print available?**
EA, EAS, EES, CSP, and Web Direct.

**Q46. To which customer segments is Universal Print available?**
Commercial (incl. WW Commercial Public Sector), Education, and Non-profit. US Government clouds (GCC, GCC-High, DoD) availability TBD.

**Q47. Are there any prerequisites?**
None.

**Q48. Are print jobs pooled at the tenant level?**
Yes. The included print jobs per licensed user per month are pooled across the entire tenant.

**Q49. What counts as a print job?**
A single printed document = 1 print job, regardless of number of pages, number of copies, color/grayscale, or single/double sided. For example, 15 copies of a 10-page document sent to the printer = 1 print job.

**Q50. Is a cancelled or failed print job counted?**
Only completed jobs count. A job completed but not picked up (e.g., secure badge release) IS counted. A job that failed within the Universal Print service is NOT counted.

**Q51. How can customers monitor monthly print usage?**
Via the Usage and Reports page in the Universal Print portal. Notifications are sent when consumption approaches the monthly pooled volume limit.

**Q52. Can customers use the Volume add-on month-to-month?**
Web Direct and CSP: yes, can be dropped at any time. VL customers must wait until their next anniversary or renewal date. For non-extended peak needs, Web Direct channel is recommended.

## Cross-Tenant User Data Migration

**Q54. What is Cross-tenant user data migration?**
Enables tenant-to-tenant migration of Exchange Online and OneDrive for Business user data. Available since November 1, 2022.

**Q55. Prerequisites:**
M365 Business Basic/Standard/Premium/F1/F3/E3/A3/E5/A5; O365 F3/E1/A1/E3/A3/E5/A5; Exchange Online.

**Q56. Through which channels is it available?**
EA/EAS only.

**Q57. To which customer segments is it available?**
Commercial (incl. WW Commercial Public Sector) and Non-profit.

**Q58. Can partial migrations be done?**
Yes : some or all users in a tenant may transfer their data. The SKU applies per user.

**Q59. SKU reference:**
| PFAM | Description | SKU # |
|---|---|---|
| Cross Tenant Data Migration | Cross Tenant Data Migration Fee Per User | VZ4-00001 |

**Q60. How long is the license valid?**
Valid for one user's mailbox and OneDrive migration. Single use : expires after one use or one year after purchase.

**Q61. Can commercial customers migrate to a GCC tenant?**
No. Available between commercial tenants only.

**Q62. Can a phased approach be used?**
Yes. OneDrive and Exchange Online mailbox migrations are scheduled independently. However, all mailbox data (emails, calendar, tasks) or the entire OneDrive site is migrated : partial content subsets are not supported.

**Q63. What about SharePoint data?**
Not covered by this SKU. SharePoint migration availability is TBD.

**Q64. What happens to the source mailbox after migration?**
The source mailbox is deleted (undiscoverable, unavailable, inaccessible) : including mailboxes on litigation/retention hold. Teams data and messages are NOT migrated.

**Q65. What's the difference between Cross-tenant user data migration and VL tenant-to-tenant migration?**
- **Cross-tenant user data migration**: moves existing user data to a new commercial tenant
- **VL tenant-to-tenant migration**: moves an entire Microsoft 365 tenant (licenses) to a new tenant

**Q66. Should the destination or source tenant purchase the licenses?**
Destination tenant.

**Q67. Are shared mailboxes included?**
No. Shared mailboxes can move without an additional Cross-tenant user data migration license.

**Q68. Will retention and compliance policies from the source tenant be migrated?**
No. Retention policies are tenant-specific and not migrated. Once data is in the target tenant, the target tenant's policies and user assignments apply to the migrated data.

## Advanced Data Residency (ADR)

**Q69. What is Advanced Data Residency?**
Add-on SKU for enterprise customers to comply with data residency laws. Provides data storage at rest commitments for expanded workloads including Exchange Online Protection, Defender for Office Plan 1, Microsoft 365 for the Web, Viva Connections, Viva Topics, and select Purview components. Also provides prioritized tenant migration into local datacenter regions. GA: November 1, 2022.

**Q70. Prerequisites:**
M365 F1/F3/E3/A3/E5/A5; O365 F3/E1/A1/E3/A3/E5/A5; Exchange Online Plan 1 or 2; SharePoint Online Plan 1 or 2; or OneDrive for Business Plan 1 or 2.

**Q71. Through which channels is ADR available?**
EA/EAS, Web Direct.

**Q72. To which customer segments is ADR available?**
Commercial (incl. WW Commercial Public Sector) and Non-profit.

**Q73. SKU reference:**
| PFAM | Description | SKU # |
|---|---|---|
| Advanced Data Residency | Advanced Data Residency Sub Per User | I6A-00002 |

**Q74. Typical use cases for ADR:**
- Legal or regulatory requirements to store customer data at rest in a specific geography
- Public sector or regulated industries (Healthcare, Financial Services)
- Customers with a preference or obligation for local data storage

**Q75. Is there a minimum purchase requirement?**
Yes : EVERY user subscription within the tenant must be licensed for ADR to apply:
- M365 F1, F3, E3, or E5
- O365 F3, E1, E3, or E5
- Exchange Online Plan 1 or 2
- SharePoint Online Plan 1 or 2
- OneDrive for Business Plan 1 or 2

**Q76. What data types are covered by the ADR commitment?**
- Exchange Online mailbox content (email body, calendar, attachments)
- SharePoint Online site content and files
- OneDrive for Business files
- Microsoft Teams chat, channel, and meeting messages; meeting recordings (Stream/SharePoint)
- Microsoft Defender for Office P1 (EOP + sandbox inspections)
- Microsoft 365 for the web (Caching)
- Microsoft Viva Connections (Dashboard and Feed)
- Microsoft Viva Topics (topics, data snippets, configuration)
- Purview (select services: Audit, Data lifecycle management, EOP, Information Protection, Insider Risk Management)

**Q77. Can ADR be discounted?**
Yes, under Blue empowerment through your Commercial Executive.

**Q78. In which geographies is ADR available?**
Tenants with sign-up addresses in local, single-country Microsoft 365 datacenter regions:
Australia, Brazil, Canada, France, Germany, India, Japan, Korea, Norway, Qatar, South Africa, Sweden, Switzerland, UAE, UK.

*Note: US not included (data residency provided at regional level). Singapore currently only for specific government customers.*

**Q79. Is ADR available for new datacenter regions (Poland, Israel, Italy, Spain, etc.)?**
Yes : ADR will be available at GA of Microsoft 365 for each new local region geo. For new regions, ADR is required for data residency commitments and migration from macro-regions.

**Q80. ADR vs. Multi-Geo capabilities : what's the difference?**
- **ADR**: data location at the tenant level : for customers in a specific single country
- **Multi-Geo**: data location at the user/site/group/team level : for multinational customers needing data in multiple geographies simultaneously

**Q81. ADR vs. EU Data Boundary (EUDB) : what's the difference?**
- **ADR**: for M365 customers wanting data at rest in local datacenter regions (many geographies)
- **EUDB**: commitment to process and store data regionally within the European Union : available to customers in EU and EFTA countries; no additional purchase required

**Q82. Is there an ADR SKU for Education?**
Yes. Available to Education customers via EES since November 2021. See Microsoft 365 Advanced Data Residency for EDU resources on OnRamp.

**Q83. How does the free Move program relate to ADR?**
Microsoft is deprecating the free Move program for tenant migrations from macro-region geos to local region geos. Over time, all eligible customers desiring migration to a local datacenter region will require the ADR add-on to initiate the migration.

**Q84. What was the grace period for the free Move program?**
Existing customers in local datacenter regions had a final six-month window to opt-in for free migration (November 1, 2022 – April 30, 2023). After April 30, 2023, the ADR add-on purchase is required.
